# Georgia Institute of Technology - Admissions Requirements

**Institution**: Georgia Institute of Technology
**Research Date**: February 14, 2026
**Status**: COMPREHENSIVE
**Last Verified**: February 14, 2026

---

## Overview

Georgia Tech is a highly selective public research university with significantly different acceptance rates for Georgia residents versus non-residents. Early Action is the preferred application timeline and is required for scholarship consideration. All applicants are considered for enrollment in both summer and fall terms.

---

## Application Deadlines

### Early Action 1 (Georgia Residents Only)
- **Application Deadline**: October 15
- **Decision Notification**: Mid-December
- **Binding Status**: Non-binding
- **Note**: GA residents only

### Early Action 2 (Non-GA Residents)
- **Application Deadline**: November 3
- **Documents/Scores Due**: November 17
- **Decision Notification**: Late January / Early February
- **Binding Status**: Non-binding
- **Note**: For students not eligible for EA1

### Regular Decision
- **Application Deadline**: January 5
- **Documents/Scores Due**: January 23
- **Decision Notification**: March
- **Binding Status**: Non-binding

### Scholarship Consideration
- **Critical**: Must apply Early Action (EA1 or EA2) to be considered for merit scholarships
- **Not available** through Regular Decision

### Enrollment Options
- **Summer and Fall Terms**: All applicants are considered for enrollment in both summer and fall terms. Students may choose their preferred term or be offered either option.

---

## Test Score Requirements

### Standardized Test Submission (REQUIRED)
- **SAT or ACT**: Must submit at least one
- **Official Scores**: Highly recommended; official scores are required only if admitted and enrolling
- **Self-Reporting**: Allowed initially; students may self-report scores in the application

### SAT Scores
- **Required Status**: Required
- **Middle 50% Range**: 1370-1530
- **Average Score of Admitted Students**: 1440
- **Essay/Writing**: NOT required

### ACT Scores
- **Required Status**: Required
- **Middle 50% Range**: 30-34
- **Writing Section**: NOT required

### Score Submission Policy
- **Superscore**: Not specified in available information
- **Self-Reporting Allowed**: Yes, highly recommended
- **Official Score Timeline**: Only required upon admission and enrollment

---

## Academic Requirements

### GPA
- **Minimum GPA**: No formal minimum requirement
- **Average GPA of Admitted Students**: 4.14 (weighted)
- **Middle 50% GPA Range**: 4.00-4.26
- **Importance in Admissions**: Very important factor in admissions decisions
- **Unweighted vs. Weighted**: Weighted GPA typically reported

---

## Acceptance Rates

### Overall Selectivity by Residency
- **Georgia Residents**: 30%
- **Non-Residents**: 9%
- **Early Action 2 (Non-GA)**: 8.1%

### Key Insight
Non-residents face significantly more competitive admissions, with EA2 rates being only slightly lower than overall non-resident rates.

## Application Requirements

### Application Platform
- **Required Platform**: Common Application only
- **Coalition App**: NOT accepted
- **University-Specific App**: NOT available

### Supplemental Essay (Required)
- **Prompt**: "Why do you want to study your chosen major, and why do you want to study that major at Georgia Tech?"
- **Word Limit**: Maximum 300 words
- **Required**: Yes, all applicants must complete
- **Major Relevance**: Question directly relates to field of study

## Financial Aid

### Merit Scholarship Consideration
- **Early Action Required**: Must apply through Early Action (EA1 or EA2) for scholarship consideration
- **Not Available**: Through Regular Decision
- **Separate Application**: Not mentioned; merit scholarships considered with regular application

---

## Key Takeaways for Applicants

1. **Early Action is Critical**: Required for scholarship consideration; significantly impacts admission chances
2. **Extreme Geographic Divide**: Georgia residents have 3.3x higher acceptance rate (30% vs 9%)
3. **Test Scores Mandatory**: SAT or ACT submission is required; self-reporting allowed initially
4. **Highly Competitive GPA**: Average 4.14; most admitted students between 4.00-4.26
5. **Limited Application Options**: Common App only; no alternative application methods
6. **Supplemental Essay Matters**: Only essay asks about major and fit with Georgia Tech
7. **Enrollment Flexibility**: Can enroll in summer or fall term depending on preference and offer
8. **No Minimum Requirements Stated**: Beyond test scores, no hard minimums published; holistic review

---

## Important Admissions Philosophy

Georgia Tech practices holistic admissions review while maintaining very high standards. The significant difference in acceptance rates between GA residents and non-residents suggests:
- Strong preference for in-state students (cost and retention factors)
- Non-residents must be especially strong in test scores and GPA
- Early Action applicants have better outcomes than Regular Decision
