## Test Score Requirements

### Policy
- **Test-Optional**: Yes, for 2026-2027 admissions cycle
- **Self-Reported Scores Accepted**: Yes, students may self-report test scores initially

### SAT
- **Required/Test-Optional**: Test-optional
- **Middle 50% Range (Class of 2029)**: 1500-1570
- **Source**: [Duke - What We Look For](https://admissions.duke.edu/what-we-look-for/)

### ACT
- **Required/Test-Optional**: Test-optional
- **Middle 50% Range (Class of 2029)**: 34-35
- **Superscore Policy**: Duke will superscore ACT across test dates
- **Source**: [Duke - What We Look For](https://admissions.duke.edu/what-we-look-for/)

### Test Score Policies
- **Superscore**: ACT is superscored across multiple test dates
- **Self-Reporting Allowed**: Yes, students may self-report scores
- **Test-Optional Details**: Applicants are not required to submit standardized test scores, but may do so to strengthen their application if competitive with Duke's range

**VERIFICATION NOTES**: Duke's test-optional policy continues through 2026-2027. Middle 50% ranges represent admitted students from the Class of 2029 (Fall 2025 admits).

## Academic Requirements

### GPA
- **No Minimum GPA**: Duke does not publish a minimum GPA requirement
- **Competitive Context**: Admission rate under 5% for Fall 2025
- **Academic Standing of Admits (Class of 2028)**: 92% graduated in top 10% of high school class
- **GPA Consideration**: Academic GPA ranked as "very important" in admissions evaluation
- **Source**: [Duke - What We Look For](https://admissions.duke.edu/what-we-look-for/)

**VERIFICATION NOTES**: While no minimum GPA is required, the extremely low admission rate and high percentage of top 10% admits indicate that admitted students typically have exceptional academic records.

## Homeschool-Specific Information

### General Homeschool Policy
- **Homeschool Applicants Welcomed**: Duke welcomes and actively considers applications from homeschooled and online-schooling applicants
- **Additional Requirements**: No additional requirements beyond standard application materials
- **Supplementary Information**: Beneficial to provide supplementary information about homeschool curriculum and academic structure
- **Source**: [Duke - What We Look For](https://admissions.duke.edu/what-we-look-for/)

**CRITICAL FOR CONNOR**: Duke does not impose additional barriers for homeschool applicants. Standard application requirements apply, though providing context about curriculum, teaching methods, and academic rigor may strengthen the application.

## Application Components

### Required Materials
Based on Duke's comprehensive admissions review, applicants should prepare:

- **Common Application**: Duke uses the Common Application for submission
- **School Report/Transcript**: Required (homeschoolers should prepare comprehensive transcript documentation)
- **Letters of Recommendation**: Required (typically 2 teacher recommendations or equivalent for homeschoolers)
- **Essays**: Required (standard Common App essay plus Duke-specific supplemental essays)
- **Mid-Year Report**: Required for Regular Decision applicants
- **Source**: [Duke Undergraduate Admissions - Apply](https://admissions.duke.edu/apply/)
