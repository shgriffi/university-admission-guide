## Overview

North Carolina A&T State University is a historically Black university (HBCU) located in Greensboro, North Carolina. The institution serves over 11,000 students and maintains strong academics with an acceptance rate of 46%. NC A&T offers a holistic admissions process that welcomes applications from students across all backgrounds.

## Standardized Test Scores

### Test-Optional Policy

NC A&T implements a **flexible test-optional admissions policy** as follows:

- **Students with GPA 2.8 or above**: May apply without submitting standardized test scores (test-optional)
- **Students with GPA below 2.8**: Must submit either SAT or ACT scores for consideration

### Middle 50% Test Score Ranges

For students who choose to submit standardized test scores:

| Test | Middle 50% Range |
|---|---|
| **SAT** | 960-1190 |
| **ACT** | 16-22 |

### Test Codes

- **SAT Code**: 5003
- **ACT Code**: 3060

### Test Score Policy

- **Self-Reporting**: Scores may be self-reported in the application
- **Official Scores**: Official score reports from testing agencies are required upon enrollment

**Source**: [NC A&T Admissions Criteria](https://www.ncat.edu/admissions/undergraduate/freshman/admissions-criteria.php)

## Homeschool-Specific Requirements

### Homeschool Applicants at NC A&T

NC A&T welcomes homeschooled applicants. Homeschoolers must comply with **North Carolina homeschool regulations**:

### Transcript Requirements for Homeschoolers

- **Transcript Format**: Flexible - NC A&T accepts parent-created transcripts
- **Chief Administrator Requirement**: The chief administrator of the homeschool must design and maintain official transcripts
- **Documentation**: Transcripts must document coursework, grades, and academic progress
- **Submission**: Transcripts should be submitted as part of the application

### Academic Evaluation

- **Weighted GPA Calculation**: Homeschoolers should calculate GPA using a weighted methodology (AP/honors courses counted at higher weight)
- **Rigor of Coursework**: NC A&T evaluates the rigor and quality of homeschool coursework
- **Standard of Comparison**: Homeschoolers are evaluated using the same standards as traditionally schooled students

### Letters of Recommendation

Since letters of recommendation are **not required** at NC A&T, homeschoolers are not obligated to obtain them. However, if choosing to submit recommendations, homeschool families may submit letters from:
- Tutors
- Online course instructors
- Community members who can speak to the student's academic abilities
- Employers or mentors

### Test Requirements for Homeschoolers

Homeschoolers follow the same test-optional policy as all applicants:
- **GPA 2.8 or above**: Test-optional (may apply without scores)
- **GPA below 2.8**: Must submit SAT or ACT scores

**Source**: [NC A&T How to Apply](https://ncat.edu/admissions/undergraduate/how-to-apply.php) and [NC A&T Admissions Criteria](https://www.ncat.edu/admissions/undergraduate/freshman/admissions-criteria.php)

## Enrollment and Residency

### Residency Priorities

NC A&T prioritizes enrollment diversity with the following policy:

- **North Carolina Residents**: At least 65% of available spots are reserved for North Carolina residents
- **Out-of-State Students**: Maximum 35% of available spots for out-of-state applicants

**Note**: As a North Carolina public university, NC A&T prioritizes admission of NC residents while maintaining a diverse student body through strategic out-of-state enrollment. As a NC resident, Connor benefits from this policy.
