## Application Deadlines

### Early Action
- **Deadline**: November 1, 2025
- **Decision Notification**: January 30, 2026
- **Binding Status**: Non-binding
- **Document Receipt Deadline**: November 15, 2025 (documents must be received within 2 weeks of application deadline)
- **Source**: [UNC Charlotte First-Year Students](https://admissions.charlotte.edu/apply/first-year-students/)

### Regular Decision
- **Deadline**: February 1, 2026
- **Decision Notification**: Rolling basis after February 1
- **Binding Status**: Non-binding
- **Document Receipt Deadline**: February 15, 2026 (documents must be received within 2 weeks of application deadline)
- **Source**: [UNC Charlotte Application Requirements](https://admissions.charlotte.edu/apply/first-year-students/application-requirements/)

### Rolling Admissions
- **Available**: Yes, after February 1, 2026
- **Details**: Applications submitted after the Regular Decision deadline will be reviewed on a rolling basis. Early submission recommended for better course selection.

## Test Score Requirements

### Policy
- **Test-Optional**: Yes, with GPA-based conditions
- **Test-Optional Threshold**: Weighted GPA 2.8 or higher at end of Junior Year
- **Testing Required Below Threshold**: Yes, if GPA below 2.8

### SAT
- **Required/Test-Optional**: Test-optional (unless GPA below 2.8)
- **Middle 50% Range**: 1110-1320
- **Minimum Required Score (if GPA below 2.8)**: 930+
- **Source**: [UNC Charlotte First-Year Students](https://admissions.charlotte.edu/apply/first-year-students/)

### ACT
- **Required/Test-Optional**: Test-optional (unless GPA below 2.8)
- **Middle 50% Range**: 21-27
- **Minimum Required Score (if GPA below 2.8)**: 17+
- **Source**: [UNC Charlotte Application Requirements](https://admissions.charlotte.edu/apply/first-year-students/application-requirements/)

### Test Score Policies
- **Self-Reporting Allowed**: Information on self-reporting policy available through admissions office
- **Test-Optional Details**: Applicants with weighted GPA of 2.8+ at the end of junior year may apply test-optional. Students below this GPA threshold must submit SAT (930+) or ACT (17+) scores.

**VERIFICATION NOTES**: UNC Charlotte's test-optional policy is conditional on GPA, making it more flexible than many institutions for qualified applicants but requiring test scores for others.

## Academic Requirements

### GPA
- **Minimum Required**: 2.5 weighted (UNC System requirement)
- **Average Admitted GPA**: 3.55
- **Conditional Test-Optional**: Students with 2.8+ weighted GPA at end of junior year may apply test-optional
- **Source**: [UNC Charlotte First-Year Students](https://admissions.charlotte.edu/apply/first-year-students/)

**VERIFICATION NOTES**: UNC Charlotte maintains the UNC System minimum of 2.5 GPA, but average admitted GPA is notably higher at 3.55. The test-optional threshold of 2.8 creates clear expectations for applicants.

## In-State vs. Out-of-State Status

### North Carolina Residents
- **In-State Classification**: UNC Charlotte is part of the UNC System
- **Connor's Status**: In-State (NC resident, homeschooled in NC)
- **Residency Certification**: NC residents must complete residency certification process as part of application
- **Residency Contact**: Residency Determination Service
  - **Phone**: 1-844-319-3640
  - **Source**: [UNC Charlotte First-Year Students](https://admissions.charlotte.edu/apply/first-year-students/)

**VERIFICATION NOTES**: In-state residents benefit from lower tuition and may have slightly different processing requirements through the residency certification system.
