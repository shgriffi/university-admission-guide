## Application Requirements

### Application Platform
- **Common Application**: Required (Duke does not accept Coalition App)

### Application Fee
- **Fee**: $85
- **Fee Waivers**: Available for eligible students
  - Common App fee waiver accepted
  - Contact admissions for waiver eligibility

### Essays Required

**Common App Personal Statement** (Required):
- Topic: Choose 1 of 7 Common App prompts
- Word Limit: 650 words maximum

**Duke Supplemental Essays** (Required):
1. **Why Duke Essay**: What is your sense of Duke as a university and a community, and why do you consider it a good match for you? (200 words)
2. **Short Answer Questions**: Typically 2-3 short responses about extracurriculars, community, interests

**Note**: Verify exact prompts on Duke website for 2026-2027 application cycle

### Recommendations
- **Counselor Recommendation**: Required (1)
  - For homeschoolers: Parent can serve as counselor
- **Teacher Recommendations**: Required (2)
  - For homeschoolers: Can be from outside instructors, tutors, mentors, dual enrollment professors

### Transcript
- **Official Homeschool Transcript**: Required
- Must include: Courses by year, grades, GPA calculation, parent signature
- Parent-created transcript accepted

### Standardized Testing
- **Test-Optional**: Yes, for 2026-2027 (verify policy continuation)
- **If submitting**: Can self-report SAT/ACT scores on application
  - Official scores required upon enrollment if admitted
- **Competitive Scores**: Middle 50%: SAT 1500-1570, ACT 34-35
  - Most admitted students have scores in this range

### Other Materials
- **Activities List**: Resume or activities list in Common App
- **Optional Materials**: Research abstracts, art supplements (if significant accomplishment)

## Homeschool-Specific Requirements

### Transcript Format
- **Format**: No specific format required - parent-created transcript accepted
- **Must Include**:
  - Courses listed by year (9th-12th grade)
  - Grades for each course
  - GPA calculation
  - Grading scale used
  - Parent/administrator signature

### GPA Documentation
- **Parent-Calculated GPA**: Accepted
- **Weighted or Unweighted**: Either is fine (specify which)

### Course Descriptions
- **Required**: No
- **Recommended**: For non-standard course titles

### Third-Party Validation
- **Required**: No
- **Recommended**: Yes - highly recommended given Duke's selectivity
- **Options**:
  - SAT/ACT scores (strong scores demonstrate academic ability)
  - AP Exam scores (4s and 5s)
  - Dual enrollment courses with grades
  - Community college transcripts
  - Subject-specific achievements

### Homeschool Supplement Form
- **Required**: No specific homeschool form
- **Common App School Report**: Parent completes as "counselor"

### NC Homeschool Documentation
- **NC Homeschool Registration**: Not required to submit
- **Annual Testing**: Not required to submit

### Letters of Recommendation
- **Parent Letters**: Parent serves as counselor - completes counselor recommendation
- **Outside Recommenders**: 2 teacher recommendations from non-parent instructors **REQUIRED**
  - Can be: tutors, dual enrollment professors, online course teachers, mentors, coaches (academic focus preferred)

### Homeschool-Friendliness Rating
**✅ Very Friendly** - Duke is highly welcoming to homeschool applicants; no additional requirements beyond standard application

### Special Notes
- Duke has successful homeschool applicant history
- No additional hurdles for homeschoolers
- Test-optional policy applies equally to homeschoolers
- Focus on strong academic record and outside validation

## Financial Aid Information

### Net Price Calculator
- **Link**: [Duke Net Price Calculator](https://financialaid.duke.edu/net-price-calculator)
- **Use Before Applying**: Estimate costs and potential aid package

### Tuition (Private University)
- **No In-State Advantage**: Duke is private; all students pay same tuition regardless of residency
- **High Cost**: Private university tuition significantly higher than NC public schools

### Merit Scholarships
- **Available**: Limited merit scholarships
  - **Robertson Scholars Program**: Full-ride merit scholarship (separate application, highly competitive, partnership with UNC Chapel Hill)
  - **A.B. Duke Scholars**: Merit scholarship for top students
  - **University Scholars Program**: Merit-based awards
- **Most Aid is Need-Based**: Duke focuses primarily on need-based financial aid

### Need-Based Aid
- **FAFSA Required**: Yes
- **CSS Profile Required**: Yes
- **Duke Financial Aid Philosophy**: Meets 100% of demonstrated financial need
- **Need-Blind Admissions**: Duke is need-blind for all US applicants (financial need doesn't affect admission decision)
- **Loan-Free Financial Aid**: Duke typically offers grants instead of loans

### Private University Considerations
- **Generous Aid Packages**: Duke is known for strong financial aid for families with demonstrated need
- **Run Net Price Calculator**: Essential to estimate actual cost after aid

**Source**: [Duke Financial Aid](https://financialaid.duke.edu/)

## Application Notes

### Why Duke is a Reach School
1. **Under 5% Acceptance Rate**: Most selective school in this example
2. **High Test Scores**: Middle 50% SAT 1500-1570 (very competitive)
3. **Holistic Admissions**: Strong academics + exceptional extracurriculars needed
4. **Private University**: Higher cost (but generous financial aid if need-based aid qualifies)

### Application Strategy
- **Early Decision**: Only apply ED if Duke is absolute #1 choice and family can commit
- **Strong Application Required**: Exceptional grades, test scores, essays, recommendations
- **Homeschool Advantage**: Strong homeschool transcript with outside validation helps
- **Consider Test Submission**: If SAT/ACT is strong (1500+), submit scores to be competitive
- **Realistic Assessment**: This is a "reach" school - apply but have backup options

### Program Fit Concerns
- **No Business School**: Duke doesn't offer undergraduate business (only Economics)
- **No Aerospace Engineering**: Only mechanical engineering with aerospace focus
- **Limited Design/Architecture**: Not strong fit for industrial design or architecture interests
- **Best Fit**: If pursuing economics, general engineering, or liberal arts
