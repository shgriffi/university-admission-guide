## Application Requirements

### Application Platform
- **Common Application** OR **NC A&T Application Portal** (applicant's choice)
- NC A&T accepts either platform equally

### Application Fee
- **Fee**: $60 (non-refundable)
- **Fee Waivers**: Available for eligible students
  - Common App fee waiver accepted
  - Contact admissions office for waiver eligibility

### Essays Required

**Personal Essays**: NOT required for NC A&T admissions
- **Significant Advantage**: No essays required (reduces application burden)
- **Optional**: Students may submit essays to strengthen application

### Recommendations
- **Letters of Recommendation**: NOT required for NC A&T admissions
- **Optional**: Students may submit recommendations to strengthen application
  - For homeschoolers: Can be from tutors, coaches, mentors, community college professors

### Transcript
- **Official Homeschool Transcript**: Required
- Must include: Courses by year, grades, GPA calculation, parent signature
- Parent-created transcript accepted (flexible formatting)

### Standardized Testing
- **Test-Optional**: Yes, if weighted GPA 2.8 or above
- **Required if GPA below 2.8**: Must submit SAT or ACT scores
- **If submitting**: Can self-report SAT/ACT scores on application
  - Official scores required upon enrollment if admitted
- **SAT Code**: 5003
- **ACT Code**: 3060

### Other Materials
- **Activities List**: Part of application (extracurriculars, awards, work experience)
- **No Portfolio**: Not required

## Homeschool-Specific Requirements

### Homeschool Policy
**✅ VERY FRIENDLY** - NC A&T welcomes homeschooled applicants with no additional requirements beyond standard materials.

### Transcript Format
- **Format**: Flexible - NC A&T accepts parent-created transcripts
- **Chief Administrator Requirement**: The chief administrator of the homeschool (parent) must design and maintain official transcripts
- **Must Include**:
  - Courses listed by year (9th-12th grade)
  - Grades for each course
  - GPA calculation (weighted recommended)
  - Grading scale used
  - Parent/administrator signature

### GPA Documentation
- **Parent-Calculated GPA**: Accepted
- **Weighted GPA**: Recommended - use weighted methodology (AP/honors courses counted at higher weight)
- **Rigor Evaluation**: NC A&T evaluates rigor and quality of homeschool coursework
- **Standard of Comparison**: Homeschoolers are evaluated using the same standards as traditionally schooled students

### Course Descriptions
- **Required**: No
- **Recommended**: For non-standard course titles

### Third-Party Validation
- **NOT Required**: NC A&T does NOT require third-party validation of coursework
- **Recommended**: Yes - strengthens application
- **Options**:
  - SAT/ACT scores (if submitting)
  - AP Exam scores
  - Dual enrollment courses with grades
  - Community college transcripts

### Homeschool Supplement Form
- **Required**: No specific homeschool form
- **Common App School Report**: Parent completes as "counselor"

### NC Homeschool Documentation
- **NC Homeschool Registration**: Not required to submit
- **Annual Testing**: Not required to submit
- **Compliance**: Must comply with NC homeschool law, but proof not needed for admissions

### Letters of Recommendation
- **NOT Required**: NC A&T does NOT require recommendations
- **Optional**: Homeschoolers may submit letters from:
  - Tutors, online course instructors, community members
  - Employers, mentors, coaches, dual enrollment professors

### Test Requirements for Homeschoolers
- **Same as All Applicants**: Test-optional if GPA 2.8+
- **Test Required if GPA below 2.8**: Must submit SAT or ACT scores

### Homeschool-Friendliness Rating
**✅ VERY FRIENDLY** - NC A&T is highly welcoming to homeschool applicants

**Rationale**:
- Accepts parent-created transcripts with flexible formatting
- Does NOT require third-party validation of coursework
- Does NOT require letters of recommendation (optional)
- Does NOT require personal essays (optional)
- Applies same evaluation standards to homeschoolers as traditional students
- Clear test-optional policy based on GPA (2.8+)
- Prioritizes North Carolina residents (65% in-state enrollment priority)

**Source**: [NC A&T How to Apply](https://ncat.edu/admissions/undergraduate/how-to-apply.php)

## Application Notes

### Why NC A&T is a Good Fit
1. **In-State Tuition**: Significant cost savings as NC resident
2. **In-State Priority**: 65% enrollment reserved for NC residents (admission advantage)
3. **Exceptionally Homeschool-Friendly**: No essays, no recommendations, flexible transcripts
4. **Test-Optional**: No pressure to submit test scores if GPA is 2.8+
5. **Strong Engineering**: Mechanical engineering with aerospace concentration
6. **AACSB Business School**: Accredited business programs
7. **1-to-1 Laptops**: All students receive laptops
8. **HBCU Experience**: Unique cultural community and traditions

### Application Strategy
- **Apply Early Action**: November 1 deadline for early consideration
- **Simplest Application**: No essays, no recommendations required (easiest application in this example)
- **Test-Optional Decision**: If GPA is 2.8+, students can choose whether to submit SAT/ACT
- **Safety School**: 46% acceptance rate makes this a realistic safety option
- **In-State Advantage**: NC residents benefit from NC resident priority

### Program Fit Considerations
- **Strong Aerospace**: Mechanical engineering with aerospace concentration (good fit)
- **AACSB Business**: Strong business programs
- **No Architecture**: No architecture program
- **Limited Film/Music**: Not dedicated programs in these areas
- **Best Fit**: If pursuing engineering, business, or seeks HBCU experience

### Unique Advantages
1. **Simplest Application**: Only application with NO essays and NO recommendations
2. **HBCU Culture**: Unique experience with strong community and alumni network
3. **Engineering + Laptops**: Strong engineering with technology support
4. **In-State Benefits**: Cost savings and enrollment priority
5. **Homeschool-Friendly**: Most accommodating to homeschool applicants

### HBCU Considerations
- **Cultural Experience**: NC A&T offers unique HBCU culture and traditions
- **Diversity**: As HBCU, majority of students are Black/African American
- **Community**: Strong sense of community and cultural pride
- **Alumni Network**: HBCU alumni networks are powerful in many industries
- **Research**: Students should research HBCU culture to determine fit
