## Quick Facts

- **Location**: Chapel Hill, North Carolina
- **Type**: Public Research University (Flagship)

- **In-State Status**: ✅ Yes - NC residents eligible for in-state tuition (verify your residency status)
- **Application Platform**: Common Application

## Admissions Overview

- **Acceptance Rate**:
  - Overall: ~18-20% (highly selective)
  - North Carolina residents: ~42%
  - Out-of-state: ~10%
- **Test Scores**: Not published (test-optional policy)
- **GPA**: No minimum published (most admitted students well above B+ average)
- **Application Options**: Early Action (non-binding), Regular Decision

**Test-Optional**: Yes, if weighted GPA 2.8+ on 4.0 scale or B+ average.

## Homeschool-Specific Requirements

### Homeschool Policy
**✅ EXCEPTIONALLY FRIENDLY** - UNC Chapel Hill explicitly welcomes homeschool applicants with NO additional requirements beyond standard materials.

### Transcript Format
- **Format**: Comprehensive transcript documentation required
- **Must Include**:
  - Courses listed by year (9th-12th grade)
  - Grades for each course
  - GPA calculation (weighted recommended)
  - Grading scale used
  - Parent/administrator signature
  - Core academic coursework (English, math, science, social studies, foreign language)

### GPA Documentation
- **Parent-Calculated GPA**: Accepted
- **Weighted or Unweighted**: Either is fine (specify which)
- **Test-Optional Threshold**: 2.8+ weighted on 4.0 scale or B+ average

### Course Descriptions
- **Required**: No
- **Recommended**: For non-standard course titles

### Third-Party Validation
- **Required**: No
- **Recommended**: Yes - strengthens application given UNC's selectivity
- **Options**:
  - SAT/ACT scores (if submitting - strong scores beneficial)
  - AP Exam scores (4s and 5s highly beneficial)
  - Dual enrollment courses with grades
  - Community college transcripts
  - Subject-specific achievements

### Homeschool Supplement Form
- **Required**: No specific homeschool form
- **Common App School Report**: Parent completes as "counselor"

### NC Homeschool Documentation
- **NC Homeschool Registration**: Not required to submit
- **Annual Testing**: Not required to submit
- **Compliance**: Must comply with NC homeschool law, but proof not needed for admissions

### Letters of Recommendation
- **Parent Letters**: Parent serves as counselor - completes counselor recommendation
- **Outside Recommenders**: 1-2 teacher recommendations from non-parent instructors required
  - Can be: tutors, dual enrollment professors, online course teachers, mentors, coaches (academic focus preferred)

### Homeschool-Friendliness Rating
**✅ EXCEPTIONALLY FRIENDLY** - UNC Chapel Hill explicitly welcomes homeschool applicants with zero additional requirements beyond standard materials.

**Rationale**:
- Explicitly states NO special or additional requirements for homeschoolers
- Homeschool applicants evaluated through same holistic admissions process as traditional students
- Standard application materials and timeline apply
- Parent can serve as counselor
- Test-optional policy applies equally to homeschoolers
- No discriminatory policies

### Special Notes
- **In-State Advantage**: NC residents benefit from 42% in-state acceptance rate vs. 10% out-of-state
- **18% Out-of-State Cap**: Maximum 18% of class from outside NC (NC residents not affected)
- **Strong Academic Profile Needed**: Most admitted students well above B+ average with strong core coursework
- **Early Action Benefits**: NC residents receive earlier decisions (by December 20)

**Source**: [UNC Chapel Hill First-Year Application](https://admissions.unc.edu/apply/types-of-applications/first-year/)

## Financial Aid Information

### Net Price Calculator
- **Link**: [UNC Chapel Hill Net Price Calculator](https://studentaid.unc.edu/)

- **Estimated Cost of Attendance**: To be researched - check UNC Chapel Hill financial aid website
- **In-State Tuition Advantage**: Significantly lower tuition than out-of-state

### Merit Scholarships
- **Available**: Yes - automatically considered with Early Action application
- **Carolina Scholars**: Prestigious merit scholarship

- **Morehead-Cain**: Full-ride merit scholarship (separate nomination process)
- **Excel@Carolina**: Scholarship program for first-generation students
- **Apply Early Action**: Priority consideration for scholarships

### Need-Based Aid
- **FAFSA Required**: Yes - submit by priority deadline
- **CSS Profile**: Verify if required for need-based aid applicants
- **Carolina Covenant**: Meets 100% of demonstrated need for low-income NC students (debt-free)
- **NC Grant Programs**: Available for NC residents
- **Federal Aid**: Pell Grants, student loans, work-study

### NC Resident Aid
- **Carolina Covenant**: Full-ride for NC students from families earning up to 200% of federal poverty level
- **NC Education Lottery Scholarship**: Available for NC residents
- **NC Need-Based Grant**: For students with demonstrated financial need
- **Check Eligibility**: Run net price calculator to estimate aid

### Financial Aid Philosophy
- **Meets 100% of Demonstrated Need**: UNC Chapel Hill meets full demonstrated need for all students
- **Need-Blind Admissions**: Financial need does NOT affect admission decision for NC residents
- **Low-Debt Commitment**: Carolina Covenant ensures debt-free education for low-income NC students

## Programs Offered (Example Career Interests)

### Business
- **Available**: ✅ Yes
- **Program Name**: Business Administration (Kenan-Flagler Business School)
- **Special Requirements**: Direct admission to business school (apply as first-year to business program)
- **Concentrations**: Accounting, Entrepreneurship, Finance, International Business, Management & Society, Marketing, Operations & Analytics, Real Estate, Sustainability
- **Accreditation**: AACSB accredited
- **Note**: One of top undergraduate business schools in the country (typically ranked top 5-10)

### Industrial Design
- **Available**: ❌ No
- **Alternative**: No traditional industrial design program
- **Note**: UNC Chapel Hill does not have design programs

### Architecture
- **Available**: ❌ No
- **Alternative**: No architecture program
- **Note**: UNC Chapel Hill does not have architecture school

## Application Notes

### Why UNC Chapel Hill is a Match/Reach School
1. **In-State Advantage**: 42% acceptance rate for NC residents (vs. 10% out-of-state)
2. **Highly Selective**: Overall ~18-20% acceptance rate (selective but NC residents have in-state advantage)
3. **Strong Academics**: Most admitted students have excellent grades and test scores
4. **Holistic Review**: Essays, recommendations, extracurriculars all matter
5. **Test-Optional**: No pressure to submit test scores if GPA is 2.8+

### Application Strategy
- **Apply Early Action**: STRONGLY RECOMMENDED
  - Earlier decision (by December 20 for NC residents)
  - Scholarship consideration (Robertson, Carolina Scholars, etc.)
  - Honors Carolina eligibility
  - Professional program assurance
  - Non-binding (can still compare options)
- **Strong Application Required**: Exceptional essays, strong recommendations, solid GPA
- **Homeschool Advantage**: No additional requirements; parent can serve as counselor
- **Test-Optional Decision**: If GPA is 2.8+, students can choose whether to submit SAT/ACT
- **In-State Benefit**: NC residency is MAJOR advantage (42% vs. 10% acceptance)

### Program Fit Strengths
- **Top Business School**: Kenan-Flagler is one of best undergraduate business schools in country
- **Strong Liberal Arts**: Excellent programs in arts, sciences, humanities
- **Research Opportunities**: Extensive undergraduate research programs
- **Studio Art**: Strong art program available

### Program Fit Concerns
- **No Aerospace Engineering**: UNC is NOT engineering-focused (NC State better for engineering)
- **No Architecture**: No architecture program
- **No Industrial Design**: No design programs
- **Limited Film Production**: Not traditional film production program
- **Limited Music Production**: Not dedicated production program
- **Best Fit**: If pursuing business, liberal arts, or sciences (NOT engineering/design)

### Carolina Covenant (Financial Aid)
- **Full-Ride for Low-Income NC Students**: Families earning up to 200% of federal poverty level
- **Debt-Free**: Covers full cost of attendance without loans
- **NC Residents Only**: Must be North Carolina resident
- **Check Eligibility**: Run net price calculator to determine if students qualify

### Is UNC Worth Applying?
- **Pros**: Top public university, strong business school, in-state advantage (42% acceptance), beautiful campus, college town, meets 100% of need, Carolina Covenant
- **Cons**: No engineering programs, no architecture/design, highly selective, not good fit if interested in aerospace/industrial design
- **Recommendation**: APPLY EARLY ACTION if interested in business or liberal arts
- **Best Fit**: Business, economics, sciences, arts (NOT engineering, architecture, or film production)

### Strategic Positioning
- **Match/Reach School**: In-state advantage makes this realistic but still competitive
- **Compare to Duke**: UNC offers similar prestige with in-state tuition and higher acceptance rate for NC residents
- **Compare to NC State**: NC State better for engineering/design; UNC better for business/liberal arts
