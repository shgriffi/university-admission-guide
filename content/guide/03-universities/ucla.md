# University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA)

**For**: Homeschool Students (Class of [YEAR])
**Last Updated**: February 14, 2026
**Status**: Complete Profile

---

## Quick Facts

- **Location**: Los Angeles, California
- **Type**: Public Research University
- **Size**: ~32,000 undergraduate students
- **Setting**: Urban campus in Westwood neighborhood
- **In-State Status**: ❌ No - Out-of-state applicant (verify your residency status)
- **Application Platform**: UC Application (NOT Common App)

---

## Admissions Overview

- **Acceptance Rate**: ~9-10% overall
  - California residents: Higher acceptance rate
  - Out-of-state: ~9% acceptance rate
- **Middle 50% GPA**: 4.40-4.73 weighted
- **Average Admitted GPA**: 3.93 unweighted, 4.58 weighted
- **Unweighted GPA 4.0+**: 56% of Class of 2028
- **Application Options**: Regular Decision only (no early action/decision for UC system)

**Test-Blind**: Yes - UCLA does NOT consider SAT/ACT scores in admissions decisions. This is a UC system-wide policy.

**Source**: [UCLA Undergraduate Admission](https://admission.ucla.edu/apply/first-year) (verified 2026-02-14)

## Application Requirements

### Application Platform
- **UC Application**: Required (UCLA does NOT accept Common App or Coalition App)
- **Access**: Apply through official UC Admissions website
- **Single Application**: Can apply to multiple UC schools with one application

### Application Fee
- **Fee**: $80 (estimated)
- **Fee Waivers**: Available for eligible students
  - Contact UC admissions for waiver eligibility

### Essays Required

**UC Personal Insight Questions** (Required):
- **Number Required**: Choose 4 out of 8 prompts
- **Word Limit**: 350 words maximum per response
- **Topics**: Community, leadership, creativity, talent, educational opportunity, significant challenge, academic subject, beyond academics

**No Common App Essay**: UCLA does not use Common Application

### Recommendations
- **Teacher Recommendations**: NOT required for standard application
- **Counselor Recommendation**: NOT required for standard application
- **Optional**: Only requested in rare cases for borderline applicants

### Transcript
- **Official Transcript**: Required
- **Homeschool Transcript**: Must include courses by year, grades, GPA calculation
- **Parent-created**: Accepted
- **Submission**: Transcripts submitted after admission acceptance (self-report during application)

### Standardized Testing
- **Test-Blind**: UCLA does NOT consider SAT/ACT scores in admissions
- **No Submission Required**: Do not submit test scores (they will not be reviewed)
- **Exception**: Scores may be submitted after enrollment for course placement only

### Other Materials
- **Activities List**: Part of UC Application (extracurriculars, awards, work experience)
- **No Portfolio**: Not required unless applying to specific arts programs

## Homeschool-Specific Requirements

### Transcript Format
- **Format**: Comprehensive documentation of coursework required
- **Must Include**:
  - Complete course listings by year (9th-12th grade)
  - Course descriptions for each class
  - Grades for each course
  - GPA calculation (weighted recommended)
  - Grading scale used
  - Clear documentation of rigor comparable to traditional high schools

### GPA Documentation
- **Parent-Calculated GPA**: Accepted
- **Weighted GPA**: Strongly recommended (use UC-approved weighting)
  - **In-State**: AP, IB, and UC-approved honors courses weighted
  - **Out-of-State**: ONLY AP and IB courses weighted (honors courses NOT weighted)
- **UC GPA Calculation**: Follow UC system GPA calculation rules

### Course Descriptions
- **Required**: Yes - detailed curriculum description required
- **Content**: Must demonstrate academic rigor and college preparatory coursework
- **Recommendation**: Document all courses thoroughly

### Third-Party Validation
- **Required**: No, but strongly recommended given UCLA's selectivity
- **Options**:
  - AP Exam scores (4s and 5s highly beneficial)
  - IB Exam scores
  - Dual enrollment courses with grades
  - Community college transcripts
  - Subject-specific achievements

### Homeschool Supplement Form
- **Required**: No specific homeschool form
- **Documentation**: Provide comprehensive transcript documentation

### NC Homeschool Documentation
- **NC Homeschool Registration**: Not required to submit
- **Annual Testing**: Not required to submit

### Letters of Recommendation
- **NOT Required**: UCLA does NOT require recommendations for standard application
- **Rare Cases**: Only requested for borderline applicants after initial review

### Homeschool-Friendliness Rating
**⚠️ Moderately Friendly** - UCLA welcomes homeschool applicants but requires extensive documentation. Case-by-case review. No automatic disadvantage when proper documentation is provided.

### Special Notes
- **Out-of-State Challenge**: Out-of-state applicant (~9% acceptance rate vs. higher for CA residents)
- **18% Out-of-State Cap**: UCLA prioritizes California residents
- **GPA Requirements**: Out-of-state minimum 3.4 unweighted GPA (vs. 3.0 for CA residents)
- **Test-Blind Advantage**: No SAT/ACT pressure; focus on GPA and coursework rigor
- **Detailed Documentation**: Homeschoolers must provide comprehensive course descriptions

**Source**: [UCLA Admission - First Year](https://admission.ucla.edu/apply/first-year)

## Application Notes

### Why UCLA is a Reach School
1. **Under 10% Acceptance Rate**: Highly selective for all applicants
2. **Out-of-State Disadvantage**: ~9% acceptance rate for out-of-state vs. higher for CA residents
3. **18% Out-of-State Cap**: UCLA prioritizes California residents
4. **High GPA Requirements**: 3.4 minimum unweighted for OOS (competitive applicants much higher)
5. **Very High Cost**: Out-of-state tuition significantly more expensive than NC schools
6. **Limited OOS Financial Aid**: UC system prioritizes aid for California residents

### Application Strategy
- **Realistic Assessment**: This is the biggest reach school (lowest acceptance rate for OOS)
- **Test-Blind Advantage**: No SAT/ACT pressure; focus on strong GPA and course rigor
- **Comprehensive Documentation**: Provide detailed homeschool transcript with thorough course descriptions
- **AP/IB Exams**: Take AP exams to demonstrate rigor (scores of 4-5 highly beneficial)
- **UC Application**: Different from Common App - requires different essays (4 personal insight questions)
- **November Deadline**: No early action; all applicants apply by November 30
- **Out-of-State Reality**: Out-of-state applicants face steepest competition as NC resident

### Program Fit Considerations
- **Strong Aerospace Engineering**: Excellent program if pursuing engineering
- **Top Film School**: One of best film/TV production programs in country
- **No Business School**: Only Business Economics (not traditional business major)
- **Limited Music Production**: Not dedicated production program
- **Strong Arts Programs**: Architecture, art, design programs available (portfolio required)

### Financial Considerations
- **Very High Cost**: Estimate $65,000+ per year for out-of-state students
- **Run Net Price Calculator**: Essential before applying
- **Compare to NC Schools**: UCLA will be significantly more expensive than NC State, UNC, etc.
- **Limited OOS Aid**: UCLA has limited financial aid for out-of-state students

### Is UCLA Worth Applying?
- **Pros**: World-class reputation, strong programs, Los Angeles location, diverse student body
- **Cons**: Very low OOS acceptance rate, very high cost, far from home, no in-state advantage
- **Recommendation**: Apply only if UCLA is dream school and family can afford out-of-state costs
