## Application Notes

### Why Georgia Tech is a Reach School
1. **9% Out-of-State Acceptance Rate**: Highly selective for non-Georgia residents
2. **Geographic Disadvantage**: Georgia residents have 30% acceptance rate vs. 9% for OOS
3. **Very High Test Scores**: Middle 50% SAT 1370-1530 (average 1440)
4. **Very High GPA**: Average 4.14 weighted (competitive applicants 4.00-4.26)
5. **Test-Required**: SAT/ACT required (no test-optional policy)
6. **Out-of-State Cost**: Higher tuition than NC public schools

### Application Strategy
- **Apply Early Action 2**: CRITICAL - Must apply EA2 (November 3) for scholarship consideration
- **Strong Test Scores**: Aim for 1400+ SAT or 30+ ACT to be competitive
- **High GPA**: Weighted GPA should be 4.0+ to be competitive
- **STEM Focus**: Highlight STEM achievements, coursework, projects, competitions
- **Supplemental Essay**: Focus on major fit and why Georgia Tech specifically
- **Contact Admissions**: Call or email about homeschool requirements before applying
- **Realistic Assessment**: This is a reach school - apply but have backup options

### Program Fit Strengths
- **Top Aerospace Engineering**: One of best programs in country (excellent fit if pursuing engineering)
- **Strong Industrial Design**: Nationally recognized program
- **Architecture**: Strong program in College of Design
- **AACSB Business**: Scheller College of Business well-regarded
- **STEM Excellence**: Overall STEM focus aligns with engineering/technical interests

### Program Fit Concerns
- **No Music Programs**: Georgia Tech does not have music production
- **Limited Art**: No dedicated art major (STEM-focused institution)
- **Limited Film**: Computational Media (not traditional film production)

### Financial Considerations
- **Out-of-State Cost**: Significantly more expensive than NC public schools
- **Must Apply EA2 for Scholarships**: Regular Decision NOT eligible for merit aid
- **Run Net Price Calculator**: Essential before applying
- **Compare to NC Schools**: Georgia Tech will likely be more expensive than NC State, UNC

### Is Georgia Tech Worth Applying?
- **Pros**: Top aerospace engineering, strong industrial design, excellent STEM reputation, Atlanta location, co-op program
- **Cons**: Very low OOS acceptance rate (9%), very high cost, test-required, far from home
- **Best Fit For**: If pursuing aerospace engineering or industrial design
- **Recommendation**: Apply Early Action 2 if test scores strong (1400+ SAT) and family can afford OOS costs
