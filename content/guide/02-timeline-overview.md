# Timeline Overview: 18-Month College Admissions Journey

**For**: Homeschool Students (Junior Year)
**Example Timeline**: February 2026 - August 2027 (Customize for your dates)
**Last Updated**: February 14, 2026

---

## How to Use This Timeline

This timeline breaks down a homeschool student's college admissions journey into 18 monthly sections, each with specific action items, deadlines, and decision points. This example timeline is designed for a student applying to 8 universities across North Carolina, Georgia, and California, but can be customized for your target schools.

**Key Features**:
- Month-by-month actionable checklists
- Critical deadlines highlighted with deadline dates
- Decision points marked for key choices
- Links to relevant research and documentation
- Specific guidance for homeschool requirements

**Timeline Phases**:
1. **Junior Spring** (Feb-Apr 2026): Planning, SAT prep, campus visits
2. **Summer** (May-Aug 2026): Testing, college list finalization, essay writing
3. **Senior Fall EA/ED** (Sep-Nov 2026): Final testing, EA/ED applications
4. **RD Season** (Dec 2026-Jan 2027): RD applications, financial aid
5. **Decision Time** (Feb-Apr 2027): Receive decisions, compare aid packages
6. **Enrollment** (May-Aug 2027): Final decision, enrollment, preparation

## February 2026: Launch Planning

### Overview
This is the beginning of focused college admissions process. As a second-semester junior, the priority is establishing baseline testing, beginning SAT preparation, and starting to explore college options through research and campus visits.

### Critical Deadlines This Month
- **February 10**: SAT registration deadline for March 14 test (if taking spring SAT)
- **February 17**: Late SAT registration deadline for March 14 test

### Action Items
- [ ] Create Federal Student Aid (FSA) IDs for student and parent/guardian
  - Visit: https://studentaid.gov/fsa-id/create-account
  - Save FSA IDs in secure location for future FAFSA submission
- [ ] Begin SAT preparation with diagnostic practice test
  - Assess baseline score to understand starting point
  - Identify content gaps in math, reading, and writing
- [ ] Research target universities' admissions requirements
  - Review research files: `/research/sources/*.md`
  - Note homeschool-specific requirements for each university
- [ ] Start building homeschool transcript documentation
  - List all courses taken in grades 9-11
  - Document grading scale and GPA calculation method
  - Note advanced, honors, AP, or dual enrollment courses
- [ ] Schedule campus visits for spring break (if applicable)
  - Priority: NC State, Duke, UNC Chapel Hill, App State
  - Contact admissions offices to schedule tours

### Decision Points
- **SAT vs ACT Initial Choice**: Choose your primary test. This example focuses on SAT preparation, but you can adapt for ACT if preferred.

### Relevant Resources
- Testing Calendar: `/research/testing-calendar.md`
- University Requirements: `/research/sources/*.md`
- Financial Aid Info: `/research/financial-aid-deadlines.md`

## May 2026: First SAT & Summer Planning

### Overview
Take the first SAT to establish baseline score. This is the critical assessment that will inform summer test prep strategy. Also begin planning summer activities including college visits, essay writing, and continued test preparation.

### Critical Deadlines This Month
- **May 2**: FIRST SAT TEST (BASELINE - MUST TAKE)
- **May 9**: SAT registration deadline for June 6 test (if planning additional spring test)
- **May 16**: SAT scores released for May 2 test

### Action Items
- [ ] **TAKE SAT on May 2** (baseline test)
  - Arrive at test center early with required materials
  - Bring acceptable photo ID and admission ticket
  - Use test-taking strategies practiced during prep
- [ ] Review SAT scores when released (~May 16)
  - Compare to target university score ranges:
    - NC State: 1290-1440 (middle 50%)
    - Duke: 1500-1570 (middle 50%)
    - Georgia Tech: 1370-1530 (middle 50%)
    - UNC Chapel Hill: Test-optional (conditional)
    - App State: 1100-1280 (middle 50%)
    - NC A&T: 960-1190 (middle 50%)
    - UNC Charlotte: 1110-1320 (middle 50%)
    - UCLA: Test-blind (scores not considered)
  - Identify specific content areas for improvement
  - Assess timing/pacing issues
- [ ] **CRITICAL DECISION POINT**: Evaluate baseline SAT performance
  - **If within 100 points of target**: Continue with SAT prep over summer
  - **If significantly below target**: Plan intensive summer review
  - **If very low**: Begin researching ACT as potential alternative
- [ ] Plan summer activities
  - Schedule additional campus visits (Georgia Tech, UCLA if feasible)
  - Block out time for SAT/ACT prep (8-12 weeks intensive)
  - Plan essay writing schedule
  - Consider dual enrollment or online courses if needed for transcript rigor
- [ ] Begin gathering financial documents for FAFSA
  - Most recent tax returns (2025)
  - W-2 forms if applicable
  - Bank statements and investment statements

### Decision Points
- **CRITICAL DECISION POINT** (May 16-20): After receiving May SAT scores, determine summer test prep strategy:
  - Scores competitive (within 100 points of target) → Continue SAT prep, plan for August/September retest
  - Scores below target but show potential → Intensive summer SAT prep
  - Scores significantly below target/plateau concerns → Consider ACT as backup plan

### Relevant Resources
- Testing Calendar: `/research/testing-calendar.md`
- University Score Ranges: Individual university research files

## June 2026: Summer Transition & Essay Writing

### Overview
Junior year ends and summer begins. This is the transition month to shift into intensive college application preparation mode. Focus on essay drafting, continued test preparation, and finalizing college list.

### Critical Deadlines This Month
- **June 6**: SAT test date (optional additional test)
- **June 30**: FAFSA federal deadline for 2026-2027 aid year (though this is for current academic year - 2027-2028 FAFSA opens earlier)

### Action Items
- [ ] Finalize junior year transcript
  - Document all final grades for spring semester
  - Calculate cumulative GPA (weighted and unweighted)
  - Update homeschool transcript with all Grade 11 coursework
  - Include grading scale explanation
  - Have parent/homeschool administrator sign and date transcript
- [ ] Begin Common App essay drafting
  - Select Common App essay prompt
  - Write first draft (650 words maximum)
  - Focus on authentic storytelling and "why" rather than "what"
  - Consider having trusted reader review draft
- [ ] Finalize college list (8 universities)
  - **Reach Schools**: Duke, UCLA, Georgia Tech
  - **Target Schools**: NC State, UNC Chapel Hill
  - **Safety Schools**: App State, NC A&T, UNC Charlotte
  - Confirm application requirements and deadlines for each
- [ ] Begin intensive summer SAT prep (if continuing SAT route)
  - Focus on areas where points were lost in May test
  - Content review + timed practice tests (2-3 per week)
  - Test-taking strategy refinement
- [ ] Visit Georgia Tech (out-of-state visit)
  - Schedule guided tour and admissions information session
  - Research Early Action 2 (non-GA residents) deadline: November 3
  - Note: Non-resident acceptance rate is 9% (very competitive)

### Decision Points
None this month - focus on essay writing and test prep.

### Critical Deadlines This Month

- **August 29**: Scores released for August 15 SAT

### Action Items
- [ ] **TAKE SAT on August 15** (improvement attempt - Option 1)
  - OR register for September 12 SAT if need more prep time
  - This is second official test to improve on May baseline
- [ ] Review August 15 SAT scores when released (~August 29)
  - Compare to May baseline scores
  - Compare to target university score ranges
  - Assess improvement trajectory
- [ ] **CRITICAL DECISION POINT** (August 29): Evaluate SAT performance
  - **Scenario A - Scores Competitive**: Within target range (1200-1400+)
    - Action: Continue with SAT; register for October 3 final attempt
    - Timeline: Polish prep in 2-3 weeks for October test
  - **Scenario B - Scores Improving**: 50-100+ point improvement from May
    - Action: Evaluate whether to continue SAT or switch to ACT
    - Timeline: Must decide by early September
  - **Scenario C - Scores Plateau**: Minimal improvement from May
    - Action: SWITCH TO ACT IMMEDIATELY
    - Timeline: Register for September or October ACT by early September
- [ ] Set up application accounts
  - Common Application account (Duke, NC State, Georgia Tech, UNC Chapel Hill, App State, UNC Charlotte)
  - UC Application account (UCLA only)
  - Coalition on Scoir account (if applicable)
- [ ] Begin supplemental essay drafting
  - Draft "Why this major?" essays for NC State and Georgia Tech
  - Draft Duke supplemental essays
  - Research UNC Chapel Hill supplemental requirements
- [ ] Finalize senior year homeschool course schedule
  - Ensure rigorous coursework (advanced math, science, etc.)
  - Document course descriptions for transcript

### Decision Points
- **CRITICAL DECISION POINT** (August 29): After August 15 SAT scores are released, make final decision on testing strategy:
  - Continue SAT and register for October 3 (final EA/ED option)
  - Switch to ACT and register for September or October ACT
  - For test-optional schools, decide whether to submit scores

Senior year begins with intensive focus on Early Action and Early Decision applications. This is the final month to complete testing for EA/ED deadlines. Application components must be finalized and submitted by early October for some schools.

- **September 12**: Late SAT registration deadline for October 3 test
- **September 24**: FAFSA opens for 2027-2028 academic year (already open as of 2025)
- **September 26**: Scores released for September 12 SAT (if taken)

### Action Items
- [ ] **REGISTER for October 3 SAT** (FINAL EA/ED TEST) by September 5 deadline
  - This is the last SAT before EA/ED deadlines
  - Scores release by October 17, giving buffer before November deadlines
  - Register ONLY if continuing SAT route (not switching to ACT)
- [ ] OR Register for ACT (if switching from SAT)
  - September ACT: Register by ~early September deadline
  - October ACT: Register by ~late September deadline
  - Allow 4-6 weeks focused ACT prep if switching from SAT
- [ ] Finalize all supplemental essays
  - Complete NC State "Why this major?" essay
  - Complete Georgia Tech supplemental (300 words)
  - Complete Duke supplemental essays
  - Complete UNC Chapel Hill supplemental essays
  - Review and polish all essays
- [ ] Request letters of recommendation (if applicable)
  - NC State: Optional (not required)
  - Duke: Required (typically 2 teacher recommendations or equivalent)
  - Georgia Tech: May be required - check requirements
  - UNC Chapel Hill: Required
  - Others: Check individual requirements
  - For homeschoolers: Letters from tutors, dual enrollment instructors, coaches, employers, community leaders
- [ ] Submit FAFSA (opened September 24, 2025 - already available)
  - Use FSA IDs created in February
  - Complete for all 8 universities
  - Use IRS Data Retrieval Tool if available
  - Priority: Submit within first 1-2 weeks of opening for maximum aid
- [ ] Complete homeschool transcript through Grade 11
  - Ensure all required elements are documented (see homeschool requirements)
  - Include grading scale, GPA, course listings, notation for advanced courses
  - Parent/homeschool administrator signature and date

### Decision Points

  - Send official score reports to EA/ED schools
- [ ] **SUBMIT UNC Chapel Hill Early Action Application by October 15**
  - Deadline: October 15, 2025
  - Decision Notification: By December 20 (NC residents)
  - Binding Status: Non-binding
  - Benefits: Scholarship consideration, Honors Carolina eligibility
  - Materials: Common App, transcript, letters of recommendation, essays
  - Test scores: Optional if weighted GPA 2.8+ (conditional test-optional)

  - Deadline: November 3 (next month)

  - Test scores: REQUIRED (SAT or ACT must be submitted)
  - Note: Non-GA resident acceptance rate is 9% - highly competitive

  - Note: Duke Early Decision deadline was November 3, 2025 (already passed for Fall 2026)

  - Materials: Common App, Duke supplemental essays, transcript, recommendations
  - Test scores: Test-optional
- [ ] Begin UCLA application (opens October 1)
  - Application window: October 1 - November 30

  - Test scores: Test-blind (not considered)
  - GPA requirement: 3.4 unweighted minimum for out-of-state

## Critical Success Factors

### For Homeschool Applicants
1. **Strong Transcript Documentation**: Comprehensive homeschool transcript with grading scale, GPA, course descriptions, and parent signature
2. **Third-Party Validation**: Consider dual enrollment, AP courses, or other external validation when possible
3. **Test Scores**: While many schools are test-optional, strong SAT scores strengthen homeschool applications
4. **Letters of Recommendation**: Even when optional, strong letters from non-parent instructors help validate academic achievement
5. **Clear Course Rigor**: Document advanced coursework, honors courses, and academic challenges

### Testing Strategy
1. **May 2, 2026**: Establish baseline SAT score
2. **August 15 or September 12, 2026**: Improvement attempt with summer prep
3. **October 3, 2026**: Final SAT for EA/ED (if competitive scores)
4. **December 5, 2026**: Final SAT for RD (if needed)
5. **ACT Pivot Option**: If SAT scores plateau, switch to ACT by early September

### Financial Aid Strategy
1. **Early FAFSA Submission**: Submit within first 1-2 weeks of opening (September)
2. **CSS Profile for Duke**: Required for Duke (private university)
3. **Net Price Calculators**: Run early to understand costs before applying
4. **Scholarship Priority**: Apply Early Action for merit scholarship eligibility
5. **Appeal Option**: Request professional judgment if circumstances change

### Application Strategy
1. **Early Action Benefits**: Apply EA when possible for scholarship priority and early decisions
2. **Balanced List**: Apply to reach (Duke, UCLA, Georgia Tech), target (NC State, UNC Chapel Hill), and safety (App State, NC A&T, UNC Charlotte) schools
3. **Test-Optional Decisions**: Submit scores to test-required schools (Duke, Georgia Tech); evaluate for test-optional schools based on competitiveness
4. **Strong Essays**: Focus on authentic storytelling and "why" this school/major
5. **Timeline Adherence**: Meet all deadlines with buffer time for unexpected issues
